## Overview

This repository defines global warming not merely as an increase in atmospheric CO₂ concentration, but as **planetary circulation failure**.

Planetary circulation failure refers to the weakening and disruption of forests, soils, oceans, microorganisms, water cycles, evapotranspiration, cloud formation, rainfall, ocean vertical circulation, phytoplankton, and carbon fixation, resulting in the loss of Earth's natural cooling feedbacks.

The central thesis of this repository is:

> Global warming is not a simple phenomenon caused only by increased CO₂. It is a planetary circulation failure in which the destruction of forests, soils, oceans, microorganisms, and water cycles has weakened the Earth's natural cooling feedbacks: water phase transitions, evapotranspiration, water retention, cloud formation, rainfall, and ocean vertical circulation.

In its shortest form:

> Global warming is the state in which Earth has lost part of its ability to cool itself.

---

## Why This Definition Is Needed

Conventional climate explanations place strong emphasis on the increase in greenhouse gases, especially CO₂, caused by human activity.

This explanation is important and should not be rejected. CO₂ reduction, energy transition, efficiency improvement, and emissions reduction are necessary.

However, a CO₂-only explanation does not fully answer the following questions:

```text
Why has the Earth become less able to release heat?
Why have rain, clouds, winds, ocean circulation, soils, forests, and natural cooling functions weakened?
Why are emissions reduction and carbon accounting alone insufficient to address accumulated heat load and degraded natural cooling systems?
```

This repository treats global warming not only as an atmospheric carbon problem, but as a collapse of Earth's cooling metabolism.

## Causal Structure

The causal structure proposed here can be summarized as follows:

```text
Population growth, urbanization, and industrial development after the Industrial Revolution
↓
Deforestation, mountain degradation, farmland expansion, road construction, and urbanization
↓
Loss of carbon-fixation sources
↓
Weakening of soil water retention, plant evapotranspiration, cloud formation, and rainfall circulation
↓
Monoculture, chemical fertilizers, pesticides, and lack of organic matter
↓
Weakening of soil microorganisms, humus, earthworms, insects, and plant-linked ecosystems
↓
Rainwater is no longer retained in soils; nutrients and chemicals flow into the ocean
↓
Coastal eutrophication, oxygen depletion, and expansion of dead zones
↓
Weakening of marine ecosystems, phytoplankton, and oceanic carbon fixation
↓
Sea-surface warming, stratification, and weakened vertical circulation
↓
Decline of global heat transport, water circulation, and natural cooling functions
↓
Heat accumulates and global warming accelerates
```

At the center of this structure is the weakening and loss of **water phase transitions**.

## Water Phase Transition Is Earth's Cooling Device

Water is one of Earth's natural cooling devices.

Water is stored in soil, absorbed by plants, returned to the atmosphere through evapotranspiration, becomes water vapor, forms clouds, and returns to the surface as rain.

Through this process, Earth moves, distributes, and releases heat.

```text
Rain
↓
Soil water retention
↓
Plant absorption
↓
Evapotranspiration
↓
Water vapor
↓
Clouds
↓
Solar shading
↓
Rainfall
↓
Surface cooling
↓
Soil water retention again
```

As long as this cycle functions, Earth can cool itself.

Modern civilization has weakened this cycle by reducing forests, hardening soils, removing humus, reducing microorganisms, incinerating organic matter, draining rainwater away, turning cities into dry heat-storage bodies, expanding monoculture, and weakening ocean circulation.

As a result, water has been removed from the natural cooling cycle, and heat accumulates more easily in land surfaces, cities, and ocean surface layers.

## CO₂ Is Both Cause and Symptom

CO₂ is an important cause of global warming.

However, this framework places CO₂ within planetary circulation failure rather than treating it as an isolated cause.

When forests, soils, oceans, phytoplankton, and microbial circulation weaken, carbon fixation also declines. When carbon fixation declines, atmospheric CO₂ becomes easier to accumulate.

Therefore, CO₂ is both a cause and a symptom amplified by planetary circulation failure.

Climate strategy must therefore do both:

```text
1. Reduce CO₂ emissions
2. Restore Earth's carbon fixation, water circulation, and natural cooling functions
```

Emissions reduction alone does not restore the natural cooling functions that have already been lost.

## Main Solution Systems

### 1. Urban Cooling

Cities have become large heat-storage bodies through asphalt, concrete, buildings, vehicles, and air-conditioning waste heat.

Urban heat load can be reduced through rainwater storage, reclaimed water use, urban waterways, rooftop greening, street trees, water-retentive pavements, and ultrasonic mist cooling fans.

### 2. Soil Regeneration

Dry soil stores heat.

Soil containing humus, organic matter, microorganisms, earthworms, roots, and fungi stores water, supports plants, and cools through evapotranspiration.

Food loss, kitchen waste, fallen leaves, pruning branches, and organic waste should be converted into humus through drying, crushing, sanitation, and microbial decomposition instead of being incinerated.

### 3. Forest Regeneration

Monoculture and abandoned forests often have weak water retention and poor biodiversity.

Mixed forests, native forests, fruit forests, wild edible plants, mushrooms, nectar plants, and watershed forests can regenerate water retention, evapotranspiration, food production, tourism, and local economies at the same time.

### 4. Ocean Regeneration

The ocean is Earth's largest heat reservoir.

When surface heat accumulates and vertical circulation weakens, deep nutrients are less likely to reach the surface, weakening phytoplankton and marine food webs.

OBS, OTU, and UMS-style systems can support oxygen supply, nanobubbles, vertical circulation, surface cooling, and phytoplankton-foundation recovery.

### 5. Desert-Edge Regeneration

Desert regeneration should not begin from the center of a desert.

It should begin from coastal zones and desert edges, where humidity is relatively higher, day-night temperature gaps are smaller, desalination can be introduced, and plants are less likely to fail immediately.

Desalinated coastal water, ultrasonic mist cooling, humus addition, water-retention layers, drought-resistant plants, fruit trees, pyramid-shaped solar architecture, vertical wind power, and underground / semi-underground settlement can be combined.

## Direct Planetary Cooling

Direct Planetary Cooling does not mean forcibly cooling Earth by artificial manipulation alone.

It means restoring the cooling functions that Earth originally possessed.

Its targets include rain, clouds, winds, soil water retention, plant evapotranspiration, microbial circulation, humus, ocean vertical circulation, phytoplankton, carbon fixation, and ecosystem recovery.

Direct Planetary Cooling is not solar blocking and not accounting-based CO₂ offsetting.

It is an attempt to return accumulated heat loads into natural circulation.

## Cooling Credit

Carbon credits evaluate how much CO₂ has been reduced, removed, or offset.

Cooling Credits evaluate how much the Earth and local regions have been physically cooled, and how much natural cooling function has been restored.

```text
Carbon Credit
= how much CO₂ was offset

Cooling Credit
= how much the Earth was cooled
= how much natural cooling function was restored
```

This distinction is the foundation for moving from carbon accounting to thermal accounting.
